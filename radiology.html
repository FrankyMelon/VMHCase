Radiology

Valley Memorial Hospital offers a variety of imaging modalities designed to meet the needs of its community. VMH has licensed, experienced staff technologists to perform the exams and a Board-Certified Radiologist to interpret the findings. Patients are sent to the Radiology Department by referral from their physician. The Radiology Department is committed to providing superior service by investing in state-of-the-art equipment and retaining quality employees. Valley Memorial Hospital offers the following services.

MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) is a non-invasive exam that uses no radiation to create exquisite soft-tissue images unavailable by any other means. MRI is used to examine the brain, extremities, joints, lumbar, thoracic, and cervical spine.

MRA (Magnetic Resonance Angiography) studies the carotid artery, blood vessels in the brain, soft tissue, and the aorta.

Spiral CT (Computed Tomography) provides full-range diagnostic capabilities to examine the entire body using standard x-ray combined with a high-tech computer. CT can examine the head, neck, extremities, abdomen, chest, and pelvis. CT also is used to direct procedures such as biopsies and drainages.

Ultrasound is a safe, non-invasive technique using sound waves to produce images rather than radiation. With ultrasound, researchers can study the abdomen, pelvis, extremities (to rule out clotting), carotid artery, breasts, thyroid, and arterial and venous doppler studies.

Valley Memorial Hospital performs screening and diagnostic exams, needle localizations, and ultrasound-guided biopsies. The hospital uses general x-ray for systematic studies, portable exams, and operating rooms. We rely on bone densitometry as a screening exam used to diagnose osteoporosis.

VMH also offers mammography. Screening and diagnostic exams, as well as needle localizations and ultrasound-guided biopsies, are done here. Digital Fluoroscopy is a tool used in the examination of internal organs with tests such as:

  • Modified Barium Swallows
  • Upper GI
  • Barium Enema
  • Lumbar Myelogram
  • Arthrograms
  • GI bleeds

Nuclear Medicine studies are performed on-site. The following scans are available:

  • Bone Scans
  • Lung Scans
  • Renal Scans
  • Hepatobiliary Scans
  • Thyroid Scans
  • GI bleeds
